## The Decision Frame

<div class="kaldewei-editorial">
  <div class="kaldewei-editorial-main">
    <span class="eyebrow">Why now</span>
    <h3 class="kaldewei-mega-statement">The real question is not whether tracking is possible. It is where traceability will remove the most friction first.</h3>
    <p class="slide-lead kaldewei-editorial-lead">The Kaldewei opportunity is operational clarity: fewer blind spots around carriers, containers, and work-in-progress, and a cleaner basis for plant-level decisions.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="kaldewei-editorial-rail">
    <div class="kaldewei-rail-card">
      <span class="metric-label">Operational tension</span>
      <p>Search time, waiting time, and manual reconciliation still absorb effort across internal handoffs.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="kaldewei-rail-card">
      <span class="metric-label">Commercial task</span>
      <p>Build a pricing structure that helps Kaldewei prove the value fast instead of over-committing before the use case is clear.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="kaldewei-rail-card">
      <span class="metric-label">Design principle</span>
      <p>Keep the story crisp, premium, and minimal: one sharp use case, one measurable proof path, one scalable commercial logic.</p>
    </div>
  </div>
</div>

## Where Traceability Pays

<div class="kaldewei-insight-grid">
  <div class="kaldewei-insight-card">
    <span class="metric-label">Locate</span>
    <strong>Critical units instantly</strong>
    <p>Track containers, transport carriers, and selected production assets across agreed zones without relying on manual search.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="kaldewei-insight-card">
    <span class="metric-label">Interpret</span>
    <strong>Movement becomes signal</strong>
    <p>Dwell time, route deviation, queue build-up, and missing arrivals become usable process information, not anecdote.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="kaldewei-insight-card">
    <span class="metric-label">Act</span>
    <strong>Teams respond faster</strong>
    <p>Dashboards, alerts, and exception workflows shorten the gap between visibility and operational action.</p>
  </div>
</div>

<div class="kaldewei-flow-band">
  <div class="kaldewei-flow-step">
    <span>Inbound or internal handoff</span>
  </div>
  <div class="kaldewei-flow-step">
    <span>Zone-level movement signal</span>
  </div>
  <div class="kaldewei-flow-step">
    <span>Operational exception or delay detected</span>
  </div>
  <div class="kaldewei-flow-step">
    <span>Action triggered with context</span>
  </div>
</div>

<blockquote class="pull-quote">Kaldewei does not need a tracking story that is broad first. It needs one that is exact first.</blockquote>

## System Shape

<div class="kaldewei-system-layout">
  <div class="kaldewei-system-stack">
    <div class="kaldewei-system-stage">
      <div class="kaldewei-stage-index">01</div>
      <div>
        <h3>Capture the physical layer</h3>
        <p>Define the relevant asset classes, plant zones, and handoff points where location and status data matter.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
    <div class="kaldewei-system-stage">
      <div class="kaldewei-stage-index">02</div>
      <div>
        <h3>Build the operational layer</h3>
        <p>Translate positions into dwell logic, process states, alerts, and plant-facing dashboards that operators can actually use.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
    <div class="kaldewei-system-stage">
      <div class="kaldewei-stage-index">03</div>
      <div>
        <h3>Link proof to rollout</h3>
        <p>Use the pilot to prove one measurable logistics outcome, then expand only once the operating model and value are validated.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </div>
  <div class="kaldewei-system-note">
    <span class="eyebrow">Why this matters</span>
    <h3 class="kaldewei-side-title">The technical stack is not the hard part.</h3>
    <p>The hard part is selecting a first scope that is narrow enough to prove, important enough to matter, and structured enough to support a later commercial rollout.</p>
    <div class="kaldewei-note-divider"></div>
    <p class="kaldewei-note-small">That is why the deck should present the solution as an operating layer, not just a hardware layer.</p>
  </div>
</div>

## Commercial Architecture

<div class="kaldewei-commercial-grid">
  <div class="kaldewei-option-card">
    <span class="eyebrow">Recurring</span>
    <h3>Platform subscription</h3>
    <ul>
      <li>Workspace and user access</li>
      <li>Tracked unit or carrier volume</li>
      <li>Visibility, analytics, and reporting capabilities</li>
      <li>Optional support or monitoring extensions</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
  <div class="kaldewei-option-card kaldewei-option-card-strong">
    <span class="eyebrow">One-time</span>
    <h3>Enablement and rollout</h3>
    <ul>
      <li>Use-case design and implementation setup</li>
      <li>Mapping, zones, workflow tuning, and integration</li>
      <li>Pilot calibration and proof measurement</li>
      <li>Expansion into further process areas or sites</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
</div>

<div class="kaldewei-pricing-spectrum">
  <div>
    <span class="metric-label">Option A</span>
    <strong>Pilot-first</strong>
    <p>Lowest-risk path. Price to accelerate the first decision and learn fast.</p>
  </div>
  <div>
    <span class="metric-label">Option B</span>
    <strong>Rollout-oriented</strong>
    <p>Broader commitment, cleaner long-term commercial terms, phased execution.</p>
  </div>
  <div>
    <span class="metric-label">Rule</span>
    <strong>Proof before scale</strong>
    <p>The pricing logic should reward operational proof, not speculation.</p>
  </div>
</div>

## Rollout Logic

<div class="kaldewei-gate-grid">
  <div class="kaldewei-gate-card">
    <span class="kaldewei-gate-number">01</span>
    <h3>Choose the first flow</h3>
    <p>Select the logistics process where missing visibility is both frequent and expensive enough to justify attention.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="kaldewei-gate-card">
    <span class="kaldewei-gate-number">02</span>
    <h3>Define the KPI threshold</h3>
    <p>Agree up front what improvement counts as success: search time, dwell time, exception resolution, or throughput reliability.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="kaldewei-gate-card">
    <span class="kaldewei-gate-number">03</span>
    <h3>Translate proof into terms</h3>
    <p>Use the pilot result to decide whether the next commercial step is plant expansion, scope deepening, or a wider rollout commitment.</p>
  </div>
</div>

<div class="kaldewei-bottom-band">
  <span class="eyebrow">Commercial consequence</span>
  <p>A strong pilot should not just validate the solution. It should simplify the next pricing conversation.</p>
</div>
